Hardware Design Insights: The Wien Bridge configuration uses lead-lag phase-shifting networks (the series and parallel RC components) to feedback precisely in-phase voltages at the resonant frequency $f_c$. At resonance, the transfer function attenuation is exactly $1/3$, necessitating a non-inverting op-amp closed-loop gain $A_v = 3$ to achieve unity loop gain ($A\beta = 1$) under the **Barkhausen Criterion**.

• In **AM Mode**, amplitude scaling is performed in the multiplier stage via FET channel resistance modulation. • In **FM Mode**, the message signal changes the bias of varactor diodes placed in parallel with $C_p$, directly modulating the time constant of the lead-lag network and sweeping the carrier frequency.
